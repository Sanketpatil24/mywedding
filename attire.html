💡 Tips for Day 2

Arrive by 8:45 AM for Vidhi — rituals begin promptly at 9:00 AM.
You'll be asked to remove footwear in ceremonial areas — wear slip-on sandals or easy footwear.
Be fully seated for the wedding ceremony by 12:00 PM.
Lots of photos happen at the ceremony and reception — dress for the camera!
Pre-book your return cab — the reception ends at 5:00 PM and cabs fill up fast.
Wear across both days
Vibrant, jewel-toned, and festive colours — the brighter the better
Traditional Indian formal wear — saree, lehenga, sherwani, anarkali
Statement jewellery and accessories on Day 2
Comfortable footwear you can dance in for Sangeet
Slip-on sandals or easy footwear for ceremonial areas on Day 2
Please avoid
White or ivory dresses — reserved for the bride
Black on Day 1 — traditionally avoided at Indian celebrations
Expensive clothes for the Haldi — turmeric will stain permanently
Very casual wear — jeans, t-shirts, sneakers on Day 2
Colours too similar to deep bridal red/maroon on Day 2
Good to know

General guidance

👟
Footwear tip
You'll remove shoes when entering ceremonial areas on Day 2. Slip-on sandals or heels with ankle straps make this much easier.
🌤️
November weather
Pune in November is lovely — around 18–28°C. Light fabrics like georgette, chiffon, and cotton silk are perfect. No need for heavy layers.
📸
Dress for photos
There will be lots of photos! Jewel tones, pastels, and bright colours all look stunning in Indian wedding photography.
